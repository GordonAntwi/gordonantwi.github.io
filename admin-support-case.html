The Challenge: Chaos and Context Switching

The CEO‚Äôs calendar was fragmented by small, unscheduled meetings, making Deep Work impossible. The company's critical documents were scattered across unmanaged cloud storage, leading to constant searching and re-work.

The core problem was administrative drag: a lack of systems that protected the executive's time and ensured documents were easily found, scaled, and managed by the growing team.

Figure 1.1: Strategic Time Management. A view of the executive's calendar, highlighting the newly protected, color-coded blocks of Deep Work time, allowing the executive to focus on strategy.

My Solution: Structural Organization

1.

Time Protection: Implemented a Triage System for the CEO's calendar, blocking out color-coded "Deep Work" time and filtering non-essential internal requests to protect their most valuable hours.

2.

Cloud Architecture Overhaul: Designed and implemented a new SharePoint/Drive file hierarchy with clear naming conventions and permissions, migrating over 500 legacy documents into a searchable, scalable structure.

3.

Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs): Documented all key processes (meeting preparation, travel booking, vendor management) into three living SOPs, allowing the CEO to delegate with 100% confidence.
